# Grit and Growth Mindset Questions and Answers

## Q1: Paraphrase (summarize) the video in a few (1 or 2) lines. Use your own words.
Success isn't just about being intelligent. According to psychologist Angela Lee Duckworth, the biggest factor is grit—staying passionate and working consistently toward long-term goals. A growth mindset, believing you can improve through effort, also helps people develop grit and achieve success.

## Q2: Paraphrase (summarize) the video in a few (1 or 2) lines. Use your own words.
A growth mindset means believing skills improve through effort, practice, and learning, unlike a fixed mindset, which sees abilities as unchangeable. People with a growth mindset embrace challenges, learn from mistakes, value feedback, and view effort as the path to improvement and success.

## Q3: What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?
Locus of control is your belief about what influence your life. An internal locus of control means you believe your actions shape your success, while an external locus of control means you believe outcomes are mainly determined by luck,fate, or other outside factors.

## Q4: What are the key points mentioned by speaker to build growth mindset (explanation not needed).
1. Believe in your ability to figure things out.
2. Question your limiting assumptions and negative self-talk.
3. Create your own learning curriculum.
4. Honor the struggle and learn from challenges and failures.
