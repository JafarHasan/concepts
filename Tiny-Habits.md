## Q2: How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.
B = MAP **Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Prompt**

M – Motivation: Your desire to do the habit. 
A – Ability: How easy the habit is to perform
P – Prompt: The trigger that reminds you to do the habit. 

## Q3: Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit? (This is the most important concept in today's topic. Whatever you celebrate becomes a habit)
Celebrating immediately after completing a habit creates a positive emotion ("Shine"), which signals to your brain that the behavior is rewarding. This strengthens the habit, builds confidence and success momentum, and makes you more likely to repeat the behavior until it becomes automatic.

## Q5: What is the book's perspective about Identity?
Instead of focusing on what you want to achieve, focus on who you want to become.When your habits match your identity, they feel natural and are easier to maintain because you're acting like the kind of person you believe you are, not just chasing a goal.

## Q6: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?
- Reduce friction: Remove obstacles so the habit is easy to start.
- Environmental priming: Arrange your surroundings to make good habits visible and accessible.
- Law of Least Effort: Make good habits easy and bad habits difficult, since people naturally choose the easiest option.

## Q7: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?
- Make it Unattractive: Focus on the habit's negative effects.
- Make it Difficult: Add friction or obstacles to make it harder.
- Make it Unsatisfying: Make the outcome less rewarding or add a consequence.
