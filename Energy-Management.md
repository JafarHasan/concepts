## Q1: What are the activities you do that make you relax - Calm quadrant?
I feel relaxed when I go for a walk, listen to music, or spend time coding without pressure.

## Q2: When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?
I enter the stress quadrant when I have multiple deadlines, too much work, or don't manage my time well.

## Q3: How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?
I know I'm in the excitement quadrant when I feel highly motivated, energetic, and eager to take on new challenges.

## Q4: Paraphrase the Sleep is your Superpower video in your own words in brief. Only the points, no explanation.
- Sleep is essential for learning, memory, and overall brain performance.
- Lack of sleep reduces the ability to learn and retain new information.
- Poor sleep weakens the immune system and increases the risk of health problems like heart disease.
- Sleep deprivation negatively affects many genes related to immunity, inflammation, and disease prevention.
- Good-quality sleep becomes more important with age to support brain health and reduce the risk of cognitive decline.


## Q5: What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?
1. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule every day.
2. Avoid using mobile phones or screens before bedtime.
3. Keep the bedroom cool, quiet, and comfortable.
4. Avoid caffeine late in the day.
5. Exercise regularly and relax before going to bed.

## Q6: Paraphrase the video - Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise. Minimum 5 points, only the points.
- Exercise improves mood by increasing feel-good brain chemicals.
- A single workout boosts focus, attention, and reaction time for a few hours.
- Regular exercise strengthens memory and supports the growth of new brain cells.
- Staying physically active helps protect the brain from age-related diseases like Alzheimer's and dementia.
- Aim for at least 30 minutes of aerobic exercise, 3–4 times a week.
- Simple activities like brisk walking, climbing stairs, or household chores can also provide brain benefits.


## Q7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?
- Exercise for at least 30 minutes, 3–4 times a week.
- Take short walks or use the stairs instead of the elevator.
- Set a fixed daily workout schedule.
- Choose activities you enjoy to stay consistent.
- Track your progress to stay motivated.
